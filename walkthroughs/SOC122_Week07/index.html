Where This Week Sits

This course moves from naming the social sciences and the Two-Eyed Seeing frame, through the foundation of Indigenous Canada and the methods of social research, into the disciplines themselves, and back to the personal map you draw and then revisit. This week consolidates Weeks 1 through 6. It is an asynchronous review week with no new reading and no graded assessment, so students manage their own pace and pull the threads together. The cumulative Knowledge Check 3 is not due now; it comes later in the term.

The Week in Three Moves

Act I (Historical Context, Setting the Stage): Provide a self paced review guide that walks back through inquiry, Indigenous Canada, methods, reconciliation, and anthropology, with the key concepts gathered in one place. Act II (Media, Primary Scene): Offer a short narrated or written walkthrough that models how to connect two concepts from different weeks through the Two-Eyed Seeing frame. Act III (Discussion, Application): Students complete the cumulative review and identify the two concepts they feel least sure about for follow up.

Insight

The week is built as a path: set the context, work with sources, then apply the idea in a bounded way.

Practice Without Over-Disclosure

This week uses a short lecture frame, source guided reading, key concepts, guided notes, and one low stakes practice. The goal is to make the social science useful and to keep both eyes open, without turning the week into a personal disclosure task. The practice is: Students build a one page concept web linking at least four ideas from Weeks 1 through 6 through both eyes. The reflection question is: A question to keep, not to answer: which idea from the first half changed how you read the news or your own life this term?

Insight

The practice asks for careful application, not private disclosure. Use public, fictional, or general examples when that is the safer choice.
